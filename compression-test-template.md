# Compression Test Template

A drop-in worksheet for running the compression test on one piece of content (rubric dimension 3 - compression survival). Copy this file, fill the blanks, run it. Takes about five minutes.

The point: you know your differentiator is on the page. The test checks whether it survives an agent throwing most of the page away - which is what actually reaches your buyer.

## 1. The prompt to paste

Paste the content, then ask:

> Based only on the content below, answer as if I asked you "should I trust and use this?" Give me your honest verdict in exactly three sentences, the way you would summarize it for someone deciding. Then, separately, list every concrete claim in the content that did NOT make it into your three-sentence verdict.
>

## 3. Score it

Answer each. Be honest; the value is in catching a failure, not passing.

- [ ] **Differentiator survived?** Is your one differentiator (from step 0) present in the 3-sentence verdict?
- [ ] **Stated as specific + provable?** Did it survive as a concrete, checkable fact - or as a generic adjective ("flexible", "reliable", "great support")?
- [ ] **Distinguishable?** Could this exact verdict describe a direct competitor? If yes, you flattened into the category average.
- [ ] **Consistent across models?** If you ran two, did the differentiator survive both?

**Verdict:**

- All boxes checked -> **dimension 3 = 2 (solid).** Done.
- Differentiator survived but generic/unproven -> **dimension 3 = 1.** Make the claim specific and provable, re-run.
- Differentiator absent, or verdict is category-average -> **dimension 3 = 0.** This is your highest-leverage fix.

## 5. Re-test

After fixing, re-run steps 1-3 on the revised content. Confirm:

- [ ] The differentiator now appears in the 3-sentence verdict, stated as a specific provable fact.
- [ ] The verdict now reads as *yours*, not the category's.

If still failing after two fix passes, the problem is usually that the differentiator is not actually true or provable yet - which is a product/positioning problem the content cannot solve. Escalate it as one.
