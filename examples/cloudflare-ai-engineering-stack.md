# Cloudflare: "The AI engineering stack we built internally" (10/10)

**Source:** https://blog.cloudflare.com/internal-ai-engineering-stack/ (Apr 20, 2026)
**Genre:** Engineering / technical

## Compression test - what an agent tells someone asking "is Cloudflare credible at AI engineering?"

> "Cloudflare runs its own AI engineering at scale on the products it sells: in 30 days, 3,683 engineers (93% of R&D) made 47.95M AI requests through its AI Gateway, and quarterly merge requests nearly doubled (from ~5,600 to over 8,700/week). The architecture is specific - an MCP Server Portal exposing 182+ tools, AGENTS.md across 3,900 repos, and an AI Code Reviewer gating every merge - all dogfooded on Access, Workers AI, and Durable Objects."

A specific, provable, hard-to-fake differentiator survives intact: *we run our own AI engineering on our own platform, and here are the numbers.*

## What to copy
- **Numbers carry the claim.** "47.95M requests, 241.37B tokens" isn't decoration - it's the evidence an agent extracts and repeats.
- **A differentiator a competitor can't copy without doing the work** (dogfooding their own stack at this scale).
- **Named architecture, not adjectives.** "MCP Server Portal, 182+ tools" beats "powerful AI tooling" every time.

## The only nitpick
- The cost claim ("substantially lower costs than frontier models") is the one place a specific number would turn a near-claim into a hard one. Everything else is already there.
